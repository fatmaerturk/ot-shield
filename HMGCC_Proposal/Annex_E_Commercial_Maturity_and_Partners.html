E.6 Regulatory alignment (NIS2, IEC 62443, NCSC CAF)

OTShield's commercial positioning is anchored in the regulatory frameworks under which UK and EU operators of essential services procure OT security:

  • NIS2 (EU): the deception layer, the audit-ready alert ledger and the asset-inventory + Purdue topology directly support NIS2's requirements on incident detection, response and reporting for operators of essential services.
  • IEC 62443: the deception, monitoring and detection components contribute to IEC 62443 zone-and-conduit security objectives at the operator's site, complementing the segmentation controls that Dragos / Claroty / firewall vendors provide.
  • NCSC CAF (UK): OTShield maps onto the four CAF objectives the partner pitch already calls out: Risk (prioritised, high-confidence alerts), Protection (passive, non-disruptive deployment), Detection (early-attacker visibility), and Response (faster containment before disruption).

This alignment matters for HMGCC because the Phase 1 research tool is, in effect, a researcher-side window onto the same platform we are taking to critical-infrastructure operators under these frameworks. Findings made during HMGCC tear-downs feed back into a tool ecosystem whose commercial trajectory is set against these frameworks, not into a one-off bespoke build.
