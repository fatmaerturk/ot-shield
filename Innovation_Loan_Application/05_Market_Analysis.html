05 — Market Analysis & Exploitation Plan

Programme: Defence Innovation Loan FY26/27 Cycle 1 | Applicant: Safetech Global Limited (OTShield) | Document type: Supporting analysis (uploaded with primary application)

1. Executive Summary

The OT/ICS cybersecurity market is undergoing a structural shift from passive visibility to active deception. UK CNI operators face a 146% YoY rise in OT-targeted attacks (OPSWAT 2025), with nation-state activity tripling. Our deception-driven platform addresses a £420M/year UK CNI addressable spend and a £180M/year defence cyber adjacency, with a global TAM trajectory to $32.4B by 2030. The £700k Innovation Loan funds a TRL 6→9 commercialisation path delivering 5 UK CNI lighthouses and 2 defence reference environments by M18, generating £350k+ ARR exit-rate and a £14.5M Y7 revenue trajectory.

2.3 Demand drivers

  • Threat escalation: 146% YoY rise in OT-targeted attacks; nation-state activity tripled (OPSWAT State of OT 2025).
  • Regulation: NIS2 (EU), NIS Regulations 2018 (UK), IEC 62443 mandates, Cyber Resilience Act, MOD Defence Cyber Resilience.
  • Sovereignty pressure: UK Government push for sovereign cyber capability post-Ukraine, post-Salt Typhoon, post-Volt Typhoon.
  • Visibility-to-action shift: Detection alone insufficient — defenders need engagement, intelligence, and slowdown of adversary.

3.3 Sovereign / strategic positioning

OTShield is one of the few UK-headquartered active-deception platforms targeting OT/ICS, where sovereign UK operational control is strategically valuable for UK CNI and defence customers. This positioning — combined with the Safetech-operated Layer B threat-intelligence fabric (12,968 captured attacks, 157 actors, 20 countries) — is a moat that visibility-focused or US-headquartered competitors cannot easily replicate.

7. Defence Funding Case Alignment

This loan supports four pillars of the UK Defence Innovation funding case:
  1. Sovereign capability: UK-headquartered, UK-operated platform reduces foreign dependency for OT/ICS deception.
  2. Threat-aware UK insight: Layer B fabric continuously expands the UK-held understanding of CNI-targeting adversaries.
  3. Defence-CNI bridge: Same platform serves both defence reference environments and civilian CNI — shared threat surface, shared learning.
  4. Operationally qualified by M18: TRL 9 readiness for mission-deployable use, supporting strategic alignment with NCSC, NPSA, MOD Defence Digital.
