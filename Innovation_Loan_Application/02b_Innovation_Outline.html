Q3Why is your idea new/novel/innovative? Explain briefly how it works.~290 words
What makes it innovative, and a brief technical overview.

OTShield is the first UK-headquartered deception-driven OT cybersecurity platform that combines four capabilities into a single integrated offering for OT environments: (a) a live, internet-exposed ICS decoy fabric capturing real attacker behaviour against industrial protocols; (b) automated asset visibility with Purdue-model topology and risk scoring; (c) PCAP analysis with attacker TTP mapping across MITRE ATT&CK for ICS; (d) a productised TAXII/MISP threat-intelligence feed sourced from the decoy fabric.

How it works. Protocol-aware decoys (Modbus, S7Comm, EtherNet/IP, OPC UA, DNP3, IEC 60870-5-104) deployed on Safetech Global infrastructure attract real attackers. PCAP traffic, asset-inventory snapshots and attacker-behaviour timelines are captured. The pipeline maps attacker TTPs across the MITRE ATT&CK for ICS kill chain (initial access, execution, persistence, evasion, discovery, lateral movement, command and control, impact). High-confidence detections are exported as IOCs in JSON, CSV and plain formats, with SIEM, SOAR, TAXII and MISP push interfaces.

What is novel. Existing OT monitoring tools (Dragos, Claroty, Nozomi, Microsoft Defender for IoT) detect anomalies in network traffic baselines. They miss adversaries who use legitimate protocol commands. OTShield adds the missing detection layer: any interaction with the decoy is, by construction, malicious because no legitimate operator should ever touch a decoy. This produces near-zero-false-positive detection of adversaries who would otherwise be invisible. The threat-intel feed productisation makes this attacker-capture data commercially monetisable as a separate revenue line, distinct from the platform itself.

UK-headquartered IP advantage. All competitor platforms in this space are foreign-headquartered. OTShield is the only UK-headquartered deception-driven OT cybersecurity platform with this combination of capabilities, providing sovereign-capability advantage in UK defence and CNI procurement.
