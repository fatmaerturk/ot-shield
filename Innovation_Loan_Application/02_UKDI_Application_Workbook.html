3.1 Desirability

Cycle 1 Assessment Criteria: Desirability. Is there a clear description of the user / business need that the innovation addresses, with supporting evidence? Does it describe the impact for defence (performance, time, cost)? Are appropriate strategic priorities the innovation aligns with identified?
3.1The defence need OTShield addresses~400 words
Describe the user / business need this innovation addresses. Provide supporting evidence. Describe the impact for defence in terms of performance, time and cost. Identify strategic priorities the innovation aligns with.

The defence need. UK Defence (MOD supply chain, defence platform OT estates, the wider national-security community) faces the same exposure as UK CNI: operational technology environments are increasingly targeted by sophisticated adversaries. Visibility-focused OT monitoring relies on anomaly detection and asset baselines, which adversaries evade through legitimate protocol commands and living-off-the-land techniques. Active deception is the missing layer: luring adversaries into interacting with realistic decoy assets that no legitimate operator should ever touch.

Evidence of the need. Industry data shows a 146% year-on-year increase in OT sites suffering physical operational impact from cyber attacks (Waterfall Security 2025 OT Threat Report). Nation-state OT attacks tripled in 2024, driven by China, Russia and Iran-linked campaigns (OPSWAT ICS/OT Threat Landscape Report 2024). State-aligned actors increasingly probe protocol weaknesses of Modbus, S7Comm, EtherNet/IP, OPC UA, DNP3 and IEC 60870-5-104. OTShield's own internet-exposed live deception fabric has captured 12,968 attack commands from 157 actors in 20 countries, evidencing the operational value of deception-based detection.

Impact for Defence. Performance: deception detections deliver high-confidence detection with significantly reduced false positives because every decoy interaction is malicious by design. This increases analyst confidence and shortens time-to-action. Time: deception surfaces adversaries during reconnaissance rather than after operational impact, compressing dwell time. Cost: a UK SME-supplied platform deployed across multiple government and defence customers shares maintenance and improvement cost, rather than each user funding a bespoke build. The £1M loan funds productisation that brings unit cost down to the £20K to £75K commercial-tier price points already published.

Strategic alignment. Aligned with the MOD Defence Industrial Strategy (UK sovereign cyber capability); National Cyber Strategy 2022 Pillar 2; NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF); NIS2 Directive obligations on UK suppliers; IEC 62443 zone-and-conduit standards; and the UK Defence and Security Industrial Strategy 2021 emphasis on dual-use sovereign technology.

Engagement to date. HMGCC Co-Creation Phase 1 proposal submitted (outcome expected 22 May 2026); selected for Cyber Runway (DSIT, Plexal), Digital Catapult Cyber 101 and CyLon. International selections: Portuguese Air Force InnCyber; Germany Hubgrade Cyber.
