1. Project Title

OTShield: Sovereign UK Active Deception Platform for Critical Infrastructure and Defence Cyber Security

2. Plain-English Summary (~250 words)

~245 words
The UK's critical national infrastructure — power, water, transport, manufacturing — and its defence environments depend on Operational Technology (OT) and Industrial Control Systems (ICS) that face a rapidly intensifying cyber threat landscape. Independent industry data shows OT-targeted attacks rose 146% year-on-year in 2025, with nation-state activity tripling. Most current cybersecurity tools focus on detection after a compromise has occurred. By the time defenders see the alert, the adversary is already inside.

Safetech Global Limited, trading as OTShield, is developing a deception-driven platform that takes a different approach. Instead of waiting to detect intruders, the platform deploys realistic decoy versions of industrial control devices that mimic real systems. When attackers engage with these decoys, defenders gain early warning, capture detailed intelligence about the adversary's tools and intent, and can disrupt or misdirect the intrusion before real assets are reached.

The platform combines two layers: customer-deployed decoys inside the operator's environment, and a UK-operated internet-exposed fabric that has already captured 12,968 attacks from 157 distinct threat actors across 20 countries. Combined intelligence is shared with partner Security Operations Centres via standard formats.

The Innovation Loan funds an 18-month commercialisation push from prototype maturity to mission-ready capability, delivering five UK Critical National Infrastructure lighthouse deployments and two UK Defence reference environments by completion. The platform is UK-headquartered, UK-operated, and designed to support sovereign UK operational control of cyber defence intelligence.

3. Public Benefit Statement (~150 words)

~145 words
This project strengthens the UK's sovereign cyber capability for protecting critical infrastructure and defence environments against the most determined adversaries — nation-state actors, organised criminal groups, and supply-chain attackers. By developing a UK-headquartered active deception platform, the project reduces the UK's reliance on foreign-headquartered cybersecurity products for sensitive defence and CNI use cases.

The threat-intelligence generated by the platform — captured directly from real adversary engagement on UK-operated infrastructure — strengthens the UK-held understanding of CNI-targeting threats. This intelligence is shared with partner Security Operations Centres across UK industry and government, raising the collective defence baseline.

The project also supports UK economic resilience: critical infrastructure that withstands cyber attack avoids disruption to essential services, protecting public welfare. Skilled cyber security jobs are retained and grown in the UK, supporting the National Cyber Strategy.

4. Technical Approach (~200 words, non-confidential)

~195 words
The platform combines two coordinated deception layers. The first layer is deployed inside the customer's operational network: realistic decoy versions of industrial control devices — programmable logic controllers, remote terminal units, human-machine interfaces — that interact with attackers using the same industrial communication protocols (Modbus, S7, EtherNet/IP, OPC UA, DNP3, IEC 60870-5-104) as production systems. Attackers cannot easily distinguish decoys from real assets, so probing actions trigger high-fidelity alerts.

The second layer is a Safetech-operated UK-resident internet-exposed fabric that continuously gathers adversary behavioural data at scale. This produces enriched threat intelligence shared with partner Security Operations Centres in standard formats (TAXII, MISP).

An AI-verifier component, supported by senior advisor expertise on the team, validates the realism of decoy responses and the quality of intelligence enrichment, ensuring the platform remains effective against increasingly sophisticated adversaries.

By project completion, the platform will be operationally qualified at UK Technology Readiness Level 9, with full IEC 62443 industrial cyber security alignment, supporting CNI and defence customer deployments under standard UK data residency and sovereign operational control.
