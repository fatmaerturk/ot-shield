1. Opening Position - the structural truth

OTShield is a deception-driven OT/ICS cybersecurity platform. Built in production today (TRL 6). Live deployment has captured 12,968 attack commands from 157 threat actors across 20 countries. Founder-owned, UK-headquartered, purpose-built for ICS protocols (Modbus, S7Comm, EtherNet/IP, OPC UA, DNP3, IEC 60870-5-104).

The business has been operated on minimal cost discipline: no salaries paid to founder or team, no office overhead, only annual virtual-office and accountant fee. This is why the Innovation Outline reached the BG&F stage with ten active corporate engagements - including two paid POC pathways under discussion - while the cash position remains tight. The team has delivered substantial commercial traction with effectively zero operating spend.

"We've reached this point with deliberate burn discipline. No salaries, no overhead - only essential statutory costs. The ten corporate engagements I'll walk you through were built on sweat equity, and two of them (Tupras and Uludag Enerji) have a paid POC pathway in active discussion. The Innovation Loan is what unlocks the next phase: paying the founder a market-below salary, hiring the senior CTO, and converting TRL 6 production evidence into TRL 9 mission-operations capability across UK CNI and UK defence reference environments."

6. Anticipated Q&A

Q1. "Your cash position is 0. How do you plan to survive until drawdown (~Oct 2026)?"

Answer: "I want to be upfront - cash is tight. We've built the company on minimal burn: no salaries, no overhead, only statutory operating cost. Two paid POC pathways are under active discussion (Tupras open innovation programme - update meeting 22 May; Uludag Enerji - referral to cyber team for paid pilot scoping) - if either converts within timeline, that supports the bridge. Beyond that: HMGCC Phase 1 outcome (22 May notification), Telefonica Tech demo conversion, Northumbrian Water cyber team engagement, and possible R&D tax credit advance. I'd welcome your guidance on which to prioritise."

Q2. "Why has no one taken a salary?"

Answer: "Founder commitment signal. The seven engagements you've seen were built on sweat equity, not investor capital. We wanted to demonstrate commercial traction before drawing salaries. The Innovation Loan is what activates a paid founder salary from M1 onwards - and it's a market-below 120K, not a market rate."

Q3. "Venture Guides is tracking but not investing. Why?"

Answer: "Venture Guides was upfront - OTShield is earlier than where they typically engage. They want to see paid deployments and a demonstrable ARR ramp before building investment conviction. They've offered to support our loan application in that spirit. This is honest signal - VC engagement is a post-loan outcome, not a pre-loan dependency."

Q4. "What if no engagement converts to revenue before drawdown?"

Answer: "Our burn structure means survival cost is minimal - founder and team continue on sweat equity if needed. The Innovation Loan timeline (~Oct 2026 first drawdown) was selected precisely because we can hold the line that long. If you see structural concerns with this plan, I'd value your direct feedback."

Q5. "Your seven engagements span six countries. Why is the loan UK-only?"

Answer: "Use of funds is 100% UK by design: UK team, 5 UK CNI lighthouses, 2 UK defence reference environments. In-scope UK pipeline = HMGCC, Telefonica Tech, Northumbrian Water. International engagements (Tupras, Uludag Enerji, Akedas in Turkey; Materna in Germany; Altura in North America) are sectoral PMF evidence and post-loan revenue diversification - they strengthen repayment confidence but do not draw on loan capital."

Q6. "How will you repay the loan?"

Answer: "Repayment phase begins after the project + extension period (per Competition Doc 9.1 and 15). By M18 we exit at TRL 9 with 5 UK CNI + 2 defence references live - this is the baseline ARR generation. Repayment trajectory then layers on international diversification (Turkey via Tupras/Uludag/Akedas, Germany via Materna, North America via Altura) and possible VC follow-on (Venture Guides upon milestone achievement). Multi-channel, multi-geography repayment confidence across four regions."

Q7. "What's your biggest risk?"

Answer: "Pilot signing pace (Risk Register C1). We need first UK CNI lighthouse signed by M9 to track the loan plan, with M6 fallback. Mitigation is the multi-pipeline activity: in-scope UK pipeline (HMGCC, Telefonica Tech, Northumbrian Water), plus two paid POC pathways under active discussion (Tupras open innovation programme update 22 May, Uludag Enerji cyber team referral for paid pilot). If C1 slips, the M18 TRL 9 target is at risk - but ten parallel engagements substantially mitigate single-thread dependence."

Q8. "Are you the right team to deliver this?"

Answer: "Six existing team members (founder, senior cyber + AI engineer, cyber sec advisor, junior cyber sec engineer, junior marketing + CS lead, marketing advisor). One senior CTO hire planned M1-M3, owning workstreams 1 and 2. Live deception fabric in production today is the team's existing output - we built TRL 6 with no funding. The loan delivers the TRL 6 to TRL 9 conversion under proven delivery capability."

Q9. "Have you applied for any other government grants or loans?"

Answer: "HMGCC Co-Creation Phase 1 (submitted, notification 22 May). Selected for Cyber Runway (DSIT, Plexal). Selected for Digital Catapult Cyber 101. Alumni of CyLon accelerator. No other active grant or loan applications. No conflicting funding."

Q10. "What happens if the loan is not awarded?"

Answer: "Cycle 2 deadline 7 July 2026 is our fallback. The seven engagements continue regardless - they were built without loan funding. If neither cycle succeeds, we explore alternative paths: deeper VC engagement (post-milestone), grant applications, or bootstrapped POC revenue. The Innovation Loan is our preferred path because of the structural alignment with TRL 6 to TRL 9 transition and UK defence procurement window."

Q11. "Is the 700K loan figure flexible?"

Answer: "The 700K is calibrated to the 18-month plan with 880,567 eligible cost and 11.92% subsidy intensity - well under the 45% small-SME cap. I'd be open to your view on whether the figure should be revisited given the lean burn structure."

Q12. "Why should Innovate UK trust you with public money?"

Answer: "Three reasons. First, demonstrated capital efficiency - we built TRL 6 production evidence with zero operating spend. Second, multi-pipeline commercial validation - ten corporate engagements with no investor capital deployed. Third, founder commitment - I have taken no salary and built this entirely on sweat equity. The Innovation Loan is the right instrument because it is repayable, aligned to a defined commercial timeline, and underwrites UK industry capability rather than diluting founder ownership."

  • Frame sweat equity as commitment signal, not weakness
  • Use Venture Guides' exact authorised language ("tracking", "would be open to revisiting", "upon achievement of clear commercial milestones")

  • Position Partner as ally - ask for guidance on bridge plan strengthening

DO NOT:
  • Overclaim any engagement (no "customer", "partner signed", "pilot agreed" language unless evidenced)
  • Invent bridge revenue numbers
  • Suggest Tupras/Uludag/Akedas/Materna/Altura draw on loan funds
